# Parent Moderation Communication Templates

**Owner:** Rowan Vale, Community & Moderation Lead  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Last updated:** March 3, 2026

Use these templates when contacting parents about moderation actions. Send from support email. Do not include child-identifying details beyond what's necessary. Maintain a calm, protective tone.

---

## Template 1: Game Removed From Arcade (Policy Violation)

**When to use:** A published game was removed because it violated our community guidelines (e.g., inappropriate content, scary content, bullying language). No PII involved.

**Subject:** Your child's game was removed from the Arcade

---

Hi [Parent name],

We're writing to let you know that a game your child published to the Vibe Code Kids Arcade was removed after it was reported and reviewed by our team.

**Why it was removed:** The game didn't meet our Arcade guidelines. We keep the Arcade safe for all kids by only showing games that are E-rated and family-friendly.

**What you can do:** You can talk with your child about our guidelines — no personal info, no scary or mean content, and games that are fun for everyone. Their game is still saved in their account; it's just no longer visible in the public Arcade. They can edit it and try publishing again when it meets our rules.

**Questions?** Reply to this email or visit our Help page. We're here to help.

The Vibe Code Kids Team

## Template 2: PII Found — Parent Notification (COPPA-Sensitive)

**When to use:** A game contained personal information (name, address, phone, school, etc.) and was removed. This is a COPPA-sensitive notification.

**Subject:** Important: Personal information was shared in a game — we've removed it

---

Hi [Parent name],

We're writing to let you know that a game your child published to the Arcade contained personal information (such as a name, address, phone number, or school name). To protect your child's privacy, we removed it from public view immediately.

**What we did:** The game is no longer visible in the Arcade. It remains saved privately in your child's account so they can edit it.

**What we recommend:** Please talk with your child about not including real names, addresses, phone numbers, school names, or other identifying information in games they share. Our platform asks kids to use a screen name instead of their real name — reinforcing this at home helps keep everyone safe.

We take children's privacy seriously and follow COPPA guidelines. If you have questions, reply to this email.

The Vibe Code Kids Team

## Template 3: Account Warning (Content Filter Violations)

**When to use:** A child has repeatedly triggered our content filter (e.g., attempted to create games with prohibited content). Progressive discipline — warning before cooldown/suspension.

**Subject:** A heads-up about your child's Vibe Code Kids account

---

Hi [Parent name],

We're reaching out because your child's account has triggered our safety filters several times when trying to create games. Our filters block content that isn't appropriate for kids (violence, adult themes, personal information, and similar).

**This is a warning, not a restriction.** Your child can still use their account. We're asking you to be aware so you can help guide them toward the kinds of games that are allowed — creative, fun, and E-rated.

**Our guidelines:** Games should be family-friendly. Fantasy combat (space blasters, swords, spells) is fine. Graphic violence, scary content, personal info, and inappropriate themes are not.

If our filters are triggered repeatedly, we may need to temporarily limit account activity. We'd rather work with you to keep things positive.

Questions? Reply to this email.

The Vibe Code Kids Team

## General Guidelines for Parent Comms

- **Keep it brief** — parents are busy; get to the point.
- **Be calm and non-accusatory** — focus on what happened, what we did, and what they can do.
- **Avoid legalese** — plain language only. Elias reviews for COPPA accuracy when needed.
- **No child-identifying details in mass comms** — if emailing multiple parents, use BCC and personalize only the greeting.
- **Support contact** — all templates should invite reply; include help/support link when relevant.
